**Run → Local Pages** generates a matrix of **service × location** landing pages
in bulk — "everything the business offers, everywhere it offers it" — each built
for local search and AI answers. It's Pagerise's take on programmatic local SEO.

## Why it exists

Local AI search rewards depth and structured local signals: businesses with local
schema, consistent name/address/phone (NAP), and pages for each service-area get
crawled and cited far more often. Local Pages produces those pages at scale
without hand-building each one.

## How a page set works

<Steps>
<Step>
### Define the matrix
Open **New page set** and enter the business name, market, a list of **services**,
and a list of **locations** (plus optional NAP). The wizard previews the count —
services × locations — capped at 200 pages per run.
</Step>
<Step>
### Generate
Each cell (`{service} in {location}`) is written by the content pipeline, grounded
in the client's [Brain](/docs/guides-run/brain) and tone, with internal links.
Every page gets a **LocalBusiness + Service (+ FAQ)** schema graph and the NAP.
</Step>
<Step>
### Review
Open the set to see each page's status. A built-in **thin-content / near-duplicate
guard** flags pages that read as too similar or too thin — those land in
**Needs review** and are held back from publishing.
</Step>
<Step>
### Publish
Publish the clean (ready) pages to WordPress. Schema is delivered via the plugin
so it ships on the live page (not stripped from the body).
</Step>
</Steps>

## The thin-content guard

Bulk pages risk tripping "doorway page" spam heuristics. Pagerise scores each
generated page for word count, uniqueness, and similarity to its siblings;
anything that looks thin or near-duplicate is marked **Needs review** and cannot
be published until revised. This keeps a large set from harming the site.

<Callout type="info">
  Local Pages live on their own surface — they don't flood the normal Content
  command center, and they're reported as a separate "local pages published"
  figure in monthly reports.
</Callout>

## Tips

- Keep service and location lists tight and genuinely distinct — quality over
  raw count.
- Fill in NAP so the LocalBusiness schema is complete (phone + address improve
  local eligibility).
