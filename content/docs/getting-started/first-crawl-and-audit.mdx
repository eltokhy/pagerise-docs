A crawl is the engine behind most of Pagerise. Run one, then let the audit turn
the results into a prioritized to-do list.

## Trigger a crawl

From the site's **Overview** (or **Settings → Crawl**), start a crawl. It runs as
a background job; you'll see progress and a notification when it finishes. Crawl
depth, speed, and include/exclude rules are configurable in
[Settings → Crawl](/docs/settings/site-settings).

## What a crawl captures

For every page it reaches, the crawl records the URL and status code, title and
meta description, headings, canonical and robots directives, the internal link
graph, images, and any structured data (JSON-LD). That **snapshot** is what every
audit reads from.

## Read the audit

Once the crawl completes, the audit evaluates pages against Pagerise's rule set
and produces:

- **Site Health gauge** (on Overview) — a single score with category breakdowns.
- **Tasks** (Run → Tasks) — every issue and opportunity, prioritized by severity,
  many with one-click fixes you can apply to WordPress.

<Callout type="info">
  Re-crawl after you ship fixes — audits read the latest snapshot, so a fresh
  crawl is how the score and Tasks reflect your work.
</Callout>
