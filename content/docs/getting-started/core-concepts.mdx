A few concepts show up everywhere in Pagerise. Learn these once and the rest of
the app reads naturally.

## Workspace, client, site

- **Workspace (organization)** — your agency account. Holds billing, team
  members, and all clients.
- **Client** — a business you do work for. A client can have one or more sites.
- **Site** — a single website (domain). Almost all tools operate at the site
  level: crawls, content, audits, rankings, AI visibility, and reports all belong
  to a site.

## Crawls & snapshots

A **crawl** fetches a site's pages so Pagerise can analyze them. Each crawl
produces a **snapshot** — a point-in-time copy of the pages, their metadata,
links, and structured data. Audits, On-Page checks, Links, and AI-visibility
signals all read from the latest snapshot, so a fresh crawl powers fresh results.

## Jobs

Long-running work (crawling, publishing, generating content, running an audit)
runs as a background **job** so the UI never blocks. You'll see jobs progress on a
site's Overview and in notifications; most finish in seconds to a few minutes.

## The Brain

The **Brain** is a per-client (and workspace-wide) knowledge base — your real
expertise, methodology, results, and brand facts. Pagerise injects relevant Brain
documents into content generation so drafts reflect the client's true E-E-A-T
(Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust) instead of generic filler.

## Skills

**Skills** are one-click AI tasks you run on a site — an AI-visibility audit, a
content-optimization pass, a competitor gap analysis, and more. Each Skill reads
the latest crawl, does its analysis, and returns scored findings.

## AI visibility

Beyond Google rankings, Pagerise measures whether **AI assistants** cite the site.
It scores citability, brand mentions, schema, crawler access (robots/llms.txt),
and per-query verdicts across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AIO, Gemini, and
Copilot. See [AI Visibility](/docs/guides-track/ai-visibility).

## Plans & quota

Some tools (deeper keyword research, link suggestions, index monitoring) are on
higher plans, and AI actions draw from your workspace quota. See
[Plans & limits](/docs/reference/plans-and-limits).
