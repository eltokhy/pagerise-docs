---
title: Improve AI visibility
description: A worked plan to lift a site's AI-visibility score — audit, fix the highest-leverage signals, then re-measure.
---

This playbook raises how often AI assistants cite a site. It follows the same
levers the [AI Visibility](/docs/guides-track/ai-visibility) audit scores.

<Steps>
<Step>
### Audit & baseline
Run the AI Visibility audit. Note the score breakdown — query coverage, engine
coverage, schema, citations, local signals — and read the findings.
</Step>
<Step>
### Unblock AI crawlers
In [Site Health → Robots](/docs/guides-track/site-health), confirm GPTBot,
ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended, etc. aren't blocked. If they are,
nothing else matters — fix this first.
</Step>
<Step>
### Ship schema + local signals
Add LocalBusiness/Service/FAQ schema and consistent NAP. For local businesses,
bulk-generate service-area pages with [Local Pages](/docs/guides-run/local-pages),
which carry that schema by default.
</Step>
<Step>
### Close the citation gap
Use the **Citations** view to see which authority directories are missing, and get
the brand listed there.
</Step>
<Step>
### Publish depth for weak queries
For queries that came back **weak/miss**, publish focused
[content](/docs/guides-run/content) that answers them directly.
</Step>
<Step>
### Re-crawl & re-measure
Crawl again and re-run the audit. Compare the new score and per-engine verdicts to
your baseline; report the movement.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
  These levers compound — schema + local pages + citations + depth together move
  the score far more than any one alone.
</Callout>
